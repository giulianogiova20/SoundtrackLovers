Game Of Thrones

By Ramin Djawadi

Making Of: Game Of Thrones

Initially, the composer rejected the offer due to a contractual conflict, but it was the fusion of ethnic influences that convinced him to work on the then-unknown George R.R. Martin. “I got a call, and then a meeting was arranged. Before we got together, the first two episodes of the first season were sent to me for review. I saw them and my jaw dropped; It was already clear then how big and how complicated the series would be. After that, we got together and talked about musical concepts. " - Ramin Djawadi

Breaking Bad

By Dave Porter

Making Of: Breaking Bad

Around that time, I happened to work often with two gentlemen who were hired or were in the process of being hired for the Breaking Bad pilot. Namely, Thomas Golubić, who is a music supervisor, and Tom Villano, who was our music editor for the first several seasons. I have great relationships with both of them and had recently worked with each of them on separate projects. They both ended up calling me on the same afternoon and said, "You won't believe this pilot I just saw. I know you'd love it."
"I had the opportunity to go to Thomas' house and watch it while he was preparing for his interview with Vince to be the music supervisor for the series. I got an early glimpse at the pilot and of course, I immediately loved it. Through my relationships with Thomas and Tom, I was able to feed some music to them while they were piecing together the final versions of the pilot to show the network. Thankfully, it was music that everyone happened to like and when it came time to hire a composer, as far as I know, I was the person they spoke to." - Dave Porter

Lost

By Michael Giacchino

Making Of: Lost

“Sometimes you can be very manipulative with music, but in the case of ‘Lost’ generally, I’m just giving you how I feel about it,” said Giacchino, who won his first Oscar and his second Grammy this year for his “Up” score. “It’s because I like the show so much. So for me, it’s about transferring my reaction to the show. If you are sitting there crying, that means I was sad too.” - Michael Giacchino

The Mandalorian

By Ludwig Göransson

Making Of: The Mandalorian

"I wanted to connect with what I felt when I was a child and saw Star Wars for the first time," he says. "The only way to revive that feeling was by stepping away from the computer and using real instruments, instruments that I could play." Göransson started with a well-known one for those who have had music classes in school: the wooden recorder. "It had a really unique sound that caught me right away," he says. "So I started jamming with her for a day and those are the first notes you hear on The Mandalorian song." - Ludwig Göransson
